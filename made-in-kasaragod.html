Traditional Coir Making at Poothali Homestay

Poothali Homestay in Kasaragod, Kerala, offers guests a unique opportunity to immerse themselves in the traditional craft of coir making—a practice deeply rooted in the region's cultural heritage. Coir, derived from the Malayalam word 'kayar' meaning rope, is a natural fiber extracted from coconut husks and is integral to North Malabar's artisanal identity.

At Poothali, guests can engage firsthand in the coir-making process, which begins with harvesting mature coconuts. The husks are then subjected to retting—a curing process where they are immersed in water to encourage microbial activity that facilitates the separation of fibers. This retting period can last between 8 to 10 months, depending on water conditions.

Following retting, artisans beat the husks with wooden mallets to extract the coir fibers, which are then washed, combed, and sun-dried. These fibers are subsequently spun into ropes and crafted into various products, including brushes, floor mats, mattresses, and doormats. Notably, coir's durability and resistance to saltwater make it particularly suitable for maritime applications, such as ropes used in boat construction.

By participating in this coir-making experience, visitors to Poothali Homestay not only gain insight into a traditional craft but also contribute to the preservation and appreciation of a cultural practice that has been passed down through generations in North Malabar.

Poduval Sons - A Legacy of Excellence

Poduval Sons is a distinguished brass and bronze manufacturing enterprise based in Kasaragod, Kerala, with a legacy dating back to 1958. Renowned for their handcrafted products, they specialize in creating brass utensils, idols, statues, Gajalakshmi lamps, and urulis (traditional cooking vessels). Their commitment to crafting items as per customer demands has solidified their reputation for quality and customization.

The meticulous craftsmanship of Poduval Sons not only preserves traditional metalworking techniques but also enhances Kasaragod's cultural identity. By producing authentic brass and bronze artifacts, they contribute to the region's unique artisanal heritage. Their products are showcased across multiple branches, including locations in Kannur, Bengaluru, Thalassery, and Payyanur, further promoting Kasaragod's rich tradition of metal artistry.
