BEKAL FORT: Kerala's Largest fort.

A medieval fort built by Shivappa Nayaka of Keladi in 1650 AD

Why is Bekal a culturally significant destination?

Bekal Fort is Kerala’s largest and most well-preserved fort, offering a blend of history, architecture, and scenic beauty. Built in the 17th century by Shivappa Nayaka, it played a key role in military defense and was later occupied by Tipu Sultan and the British. Overlooking the Arabian Sea, the fort’s massive walls, secret tunnels, and observation towers make it a stunning attraction. Its cultural significance, lush surroundings, and popularity as a filming location make it a must-visit destination.

What makes the fort’s architecture and setting appealing?

Bekal Fort's architecture is impressive due to its massive circular structure, high defensive walls, and well-preserved bastions. It features secret underground tunnels, strategically placed observation towers, and a keyhole-shaped design that maximized defense. The fort’s location along the Arabian Sea enhances its appeal, offering breathtaking coastal views, especially during sunrise and sunset. Its integration with natural landscapes, including lush greenery and nearby beaches, makes it a perfect blend of history and scenic beauty.
